Itinerary Tips

  • Allow for travel time between parks (4-8 hours driving)
  • Gorilla permits must be booked 6-12 months in advance
  • Include rest days after strenuous activities
  • Consider altitude acclimatization for mountain hikes

Essential Information

Visa & Entry Requirements

  • eVisa: Apply online at visas.immigration.go.ug
  • Cost: Single entry visa - $50, East African Tourist Visa - $100
  • Validity: Tourist visas are typically valid for 90 days
  • Passport: Must be valid for at least 6 months from date of entry
  • Yellow Fever: Vaccination certificate required for entry

Health & Vaccinations

  • Required: Yellow Fever vaccination
  • Recommended: Hepatitis A & B, Typhoid, Cholera
  • Malaria: Prophylaxis recommended for all travelers
  • COVID-19: Check current requirements before travel
  • Altitude Sickness: Possible in mountainous regions

What to Pack for Uganda

Clothing

  • Lightweight, breathable clothing
  • Long sleeves and pants for evenings
  • Waterproof jacket
  • Sturdy hiking boots
  • Hat and sunglasses

Essentials

  • Insect repellent with DEET
  • Sunscreen (SPF 30+)
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Power adapter (UK Type G)
  • Binoculars for wildlife viewing

Safety Tips

  • Always follow park ranger instructions
  • Maintain safe distance from wildlife (especially primates)
  • Drink only bottled or purified water
  • Use registered tour operators and guides
  • Keep valuables secure and avoid displaying wealth
  • Purchase comprehensive travel insurance

Budget Planning Guide

Budget Traveler

$80-120/day
  • Camping or basic accommodation
  • Public transportation
  • Self-catering or local eateries
  • Fewer paid activities
  • No gorilla trekking (permit cost)

Mid-range Traveler

$150-300/day
  • Comfortable lodges or hotels
  • Private vehicle with driver
  • Meals included at accommodation
  • Multiple park visits
  • Possible gorilla trekking

Luxury Traveler

$400+/day
  • High-end lodges and camps
  • Fly-in safaris available
  • All meals and drinks included
  • Private guides and exclusive experiences
  • Gorilla trekking and all activities

Money-Saving Tips

  • Travel in groups: Share vehicle and guide costs
  • Visit in wet season: Lower accommodation rates
  • Book early: Better rates for gorilla permits and flights
  • Use local guides: Often more affordable than international companies
  • Combine parks: Reduces transportation costs between destinations

Getting Around Uganda

Self-drive

Cost: $50-100/day

  • Maximum flexibility
  • 4x4 vehicle required for parks
  • International driving permit needed
  • Driving is on the left side

Private Driver

Cost: $100-200/day

  • Includes vehicle and driver
  • Local knowledge and navigation
  • Reduces stress of driving
  • Can arrange park entry

Domestic Flights

Cost: $150-400 per sector

  • Saves time on long distances
  • Aerial views of landscapes
  • Limited to major parks
  • Luggage restrictions apply

Public Transport

Cost: $5-20 per journey

  • Very affordable
  • Connect with local people
  • Unreliable schedules
  • Not suitable for park access
